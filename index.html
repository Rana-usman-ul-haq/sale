ERC-20 is a standard used for creating and implementing tokens on the Ethereum blockchain. These tokens are digital assets that can represent fungible assets like cryptocurrencies, loyalty points, or other assets. They are popular because they are compatible with any wallet or exchange that supports the ERC-20 standard and are easy to create and deploy. ERC-20 tokens can also be used in smart contracts, making them suitable for a range of decentralized applications. To be an ERC-20 token, it must meet specific criteria, including having a set of functions that enable transfer, approval, and querying. Overall, ERC-20 tokens play a crucial role in the Ethereum ecosystem and have enabled various innovative applications, such as DeFi protocols and gaming platforms.
